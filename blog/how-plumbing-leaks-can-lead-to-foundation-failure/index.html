Where These Leaks Usually Come From

Not all leaks are equal when it comes to structural impact. Some create slow, creeping problems others lead to sudden and dramatic shifts. Here are a few of the most common culprits we see in the field:

  • Interior supply lines under concrete slabs, often referred to as slab leaks, are a frequent cause of damage in older homes. These pipes can corrode or shift over time, eventually cracking or leaking directly beneath the structure.
  • Irrigation lines outside the home may seem harmless, but a slow underground leak can oversaturate localized soil zones near the structure.
  • Sewer lines running beneath or next to the foundation can rupture due to age, poor construction, root intrusion, or ground movement. Unlike pressurized lines, sewer leaks often go unnoticed until soil movement becomes visible or the line stops performing its function.
  • Drain lines from kitchens, laundry rooms, or bathrooms can leak slowly over time. Poor original installation or material fatigue are often to blame.
