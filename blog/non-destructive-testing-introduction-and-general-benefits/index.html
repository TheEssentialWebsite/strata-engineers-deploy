What Is Non-Destructive Testing?

Non-destructive testing is exactly what it sounds like: a way to evaluate materials without damaging them. For concrete structures this might mean using rebound hammers, ultrasonic pulse velocity tools, or rebar location equipment to assess internal conditions and strength.

Why Use NDT on Concrete?

There are several advantages to using NDT, especially when you're trying to understand an existing structure without committing to demolition of structural elements. Hereâ€™s why many of our clients including property managers, contractors, and fellow engineers call us in for these evaluations:

  • Consistent Testing Across the Structure: Need to check multiple spots to verify uniformity? NDT makes that easy and affordable.
  • Versatile Applications: Whether you're evaluating fire damage, report of too much water added in a concrete mix, freeze-thaw deterioration, or general wear and tear, NDT can help you zero in on the problem areas.
